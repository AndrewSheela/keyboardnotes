Minor Chord Formation

Learning how to build a Minor Chord using scale notes

What Is a Minor Chord?

A minor chord is a chord that has a sad, emotional, or serious sound.

Minor chords are widely used to express emotion, depth, and tension in music.

Minor Chord

A minor chord is formed by lowering the 3rd note of a Major chord by one half step.

This small change creates a big difference in sound and feeling.

Minor Chord Formula

1   –   ♭3   –   5

♭3 means the third note is lowered by a half step

Example: C Minor Chord Formation

First, write the C Major scale:

C   D   E   F   G   A   B   C

Lower the 3rd note (E) by one half step:

E → E♭

Now select 1 – ♭3 – 5:

C   –   E♭   –   G

Important Theory Notes

• Minor chords are also triads (three-note chords).

• The lowered third is called the minor third.

• Minor chords are common in sad, emotional, and reflective music.

Understanding minor chords helps you play songs with deeper expression and feeling.

Why Minor Chords Matter

Minor chords are essential for emotional expression, songwriting, and accompaniment.

Mastering minor chord formation allows you to play music with feeling and depth.
